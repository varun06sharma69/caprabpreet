5 Tax-Saving Strategies Every NRI Should Know

Many NRIs pay far more tax than necessary simply because they are unaware of the legal provisions available to them. Here are five proven strategies to reduce your tax liability.

1. Claim DTAA Benefits Properly

Submit a Tax Residency Certificate and Form 10F to your Indian bank before April each year. This alone can reduce TDS on interest from 30% to 10–15% depending on the treaty country. Read our detailed guide on understanding DTAA benefits for NRIs to learn exactly how to claim this relief.

2. Use NRE Account for Foreign Earnings

NRE accounts are fully tax-free in India. Interest earned on NRE FDs is exempt from Indian tax. Park your foreign earnings here, not in NRO accounts, to avoid unnecessary TDS. The Reserve Bank of India governs NRE/NRO account rules under FEMA.

3. Optimize Capital Gains with Indexation

For property held over 2 years, the indexed cost significantly reduces your taxable gain. Combine this with Section 54 reinvestment to potentially bring your capital gains tax to zero. See our full NRI property sale tax guide for step-by-step calculations.

4. Section 80C Deductions Still Apply

NRIs can still claim Section 80C deductions of up to ₹1.5 lakh through ELSS mutual funds, home loan principal, and life insurance premiums paid on Indian policies. File your ITR correctly on the Income Tax e-filing portal to claim these deductions.

5. File ITR Even When Below Exemption Limit

If TDS was deducted on your Indian income, filing an ITR is the only way to claim a refund. Many NRIs lose thousands of rupees annually by not filing. Also understand how your residency status determines your filing obligations.
