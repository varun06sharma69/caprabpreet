Income Tax Notices & Case Handling

Received a notice from the Income Tax Department? Don't panic — every notice type demands a specific, timely response. Our expert team has successfully handled 150+ cases across all categories. Here is a complete breakdown of what each notice means and what you must do:

Section 139(9)
Defective Return Notice
Purpose

Issued when your return is missing mandatory information or contains errors that render it incomplete or invalid under law.

⏱

Action: You have 15 days to rectify the defects and resubmit a corrected return — else it is treated as never filed.

Section 142(1)
Inquiry Notice Before Assessment
Purpose

Requests additional documents or accounts to verify claims before the formal assessment order is passed.

📂

Action: Supply all requested documents by the specified deadline. Non-compliance attracts penalty under Section 271(1)(b).

Section 143(1)
Letter of Intimation
Purpose

Automated notice after return processing, highlighting mathematical errors or mismatches with Form 26AS / AIS data.

✅

Action: Verify whether no change, refund, or tax demand is stated. Accept or raise objection within the stipulated period.

Section 143(2)
Detailed Scrutiny Notice
Purpose

Issued when your return is selected for comprehensive examination by the Income Tax Department — your entire return is under review.

📋

Action: Produce complete books of accounts and proofs of all claims before the Assessing Officer. Professional CA representation is strongly advised.

Section 148
Income Escaping Assessment
Purpose

Served when the department suspects income has escaped assessment in previous years — can cover up to 6 years back.

⚠️

Action: Preceded by a Section 148A show-cause notice. You must file a fresh return for the relevant year. Seek immediate expert help.

Section 156
Notice of Demand
Purpose

Formally orders payment of a calculated tax deficit, interest, fine, or penalty determined by the Assessing Officer.

💳

Action: Clear dues on the portal within 30 days. Non-payment triggers penalty, interest accrual, and recovery proceedings.

Section 245
Set-Off of Refund Against Demand
Purpose

Sent when you are owed a refund but have outstanding dues from previous assessment years that the department intends to adjust.

🔄

Action: Agree or disagree within 30 days — else the system automatically offsets your refund against the old demand.

Section 154
Rectification Notice
Purpose

Triggered when a mistake apparent from the record needs correction — can be initiated by either the taxpayer or the department.

✏️

Action: File a rectification request to fix the mistake without a full revision of the entire assessment order.

Section 131
Summons for Personal Appearance
Purpose

Issued to compel your attendance before the Income Tax Officer or to produce physical evidence and documents under oath.

🚨

Action: Compulsory legal summons — ignoring leads to heavy fines and legal proceedings. Always attend with a qualified CA or advocate.

Section 133(6)
General Inquiry Notice
Purpose

Issued to gather transaction records and verified data from you or third parties such as banks, brokers, or property registrars.

📊

Action: Provide the specific financial transaction records called for within the stipulated time to avoid adverse inference.

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to your common questions about taxation and financial advisory

NRI (Non-Resident Indian) status is determined based on the number of days you spend in India during a financial year. Generally, if you spend less than 182 days in India, you're considered an NRI. This status significantly impacts what income is taxable in India. Only income earned from Indian sources or received/accrued in India is typically taxable. We help you determine your status accurately and optimize your tax position accordingly.

DTAA (Double Taxation Avoidance Agreement) is a treaty between India and another country that prevents you from paying taxes twice on the same income. If you have income from multiple countries, DTAA benefits allow you to claim credits or exemptions in one country for taxes paid in another. We specialize in DTAA optimization and can help you structure your income to maximize these benefits and reduce your overall tax burden.

Fund repatriation requires proper documentation including Form 15CA (for amounts up to Rs. 50 lakhs) or Form 15CB (for amounts above Rs. 50 lakhs). We handle the complete process including tax computation, form preparation, and bank liaison. Our expertise ensures compliant and tax-efficient repatriation of your funds with minimal hassle and maximum savings.

Property sales are subject to capital gains tax. The holding period determines if gains are long-term (taxed at special rates with indexation) or short-term (taxed as ordinary income). TDS of 20% is deducted at source. Form 15CA/15CB is required for repatriation. We provide complete tax planning to minimize your liability and ensure smooth documentation throughout the process.

Income tax notices require prompt and careful response. Don't ignore them as this can have serious consequences. We analyze the notice, gather necessary documentation, prepare detailed responses, and represent you before the Income Tax Officer. Our experience in handling 150+ notice cases ensures the best possible outcome for your situation.
